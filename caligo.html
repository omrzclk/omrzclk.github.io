Omer Ozcelik

Caligo

2019

Caligo is a visual project that includes series of render images inspired by “Mimicry and Legendary Psychasthenia” article written by Roger Caillois.


"Dark space envelops me on all sides and penetrates me much deeper than light space, the distinction between inside and outside and consequently the sense organs as well, insofar as they are designed for external perception, here play only a totally modest role.” (Caillois & Shepley, 1984, p. 30)


Mimicry indicates to Nature and there is no accidential resemblance in Nature. Resemblance and Distinction appears in Nature, and it always relates with reality.


“He tries to look at himself from any point whatever in space. He feels himself becoming space, dark space where things cannot be put. He is similar, not similar to something, but just similar. And he invents spaces of which he is ‘the convulsive possession.’” (Caillois & Shepley, 1984, p. 30)


Caligo project creates own space by turning own similarity. The final image is generated by own source image that resembles and distinguish at the same time.
